Performance Enhancement of Software Defined Cellular 5G and Internet-of-Things Networks

Speaker: Furqan Khan
Seminar Date: Fri, 24/05/2019 - 11:00
Venue: 49-502; AEB Seminar Room
Host: Assoc Prof Marius Portmann

Seminar Type:  PhD Thesis Review

Abstract: 

Increasing traffic demand in the future cellular and Internet-of-Things (IoT) networks makes it hard for network operators to devise well-organized traffic load distribution mechanisms. This has led to the inefficient use of available resources due to which the network becomes unable to satisfy end user requirements. Optimal utilization of resources in emerging future networks requires extremely fine-grained control of wide-scale traffic. 

In this research, we propose traffic distribution mechanisms that enable service providers to enhance the application quality of service (QoS) in future mobile networks. Using Software-Defined Networking (SDN) concepts, two different technologies for cellular and IoT networks are explored, namely the long-term evolution (3GPP-LTE) and long-range wide area network (LoRaWAN). For each of these technologies, new traffic management schemes were evaluated using the system-level simulation environment considering different use cases. The proposed schemes give fairly better performance compared to the other state-of-the-art approaches in achieving resource efficient load distribution. We expect that the resulting network design and the proposed algorithms will ease the standardization of SDN-enabled 5G networks.

Biography: 

Furqan Khan received his B.Sc. in Electrical (Telecommunication) Engineering from COMSATS IIT, Pakistan and M.Sc. in Computer Engineering from Ajou University, South Korea. Currently, he is currently a Ph.D. candidate in the School of Information Technology and Electrical Engineering at UQ.
